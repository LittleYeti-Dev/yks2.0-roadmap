# Capstone White Paper — Zero-Trust Across the SKIPJACK Stack

_The cross-series synthesis. Each product series solves a real, peer-reviewed problem; this capstone shows they are one doctrine — zero-trust — applied at every layer of an agentic system, and argues that doctrine is becoming the precondition to operate at all._

## Thesis
Zero-trust is not a feature of one SKIPJACK product; it is the single bar every product is built to, enforced once at the substrate and inherited everywhere. We apply it across four planes — **data, actor, behavior, and provenance** — and conclude it is a commercial inevitability, not a federal premium.

## 1. Zero-trust on DATA (FUNDAMENTUM / KAIROS)
Trust no datum because of where it sits — trust it only with its proven age and custody. LLM agents are temporally blind and act on stale state with confident-wrong results ([2510.23853](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.23853v2)); miscalibrated confidence is undetectable to users ([2402.07632](https://arxiv.org/html/2402.07632v4)); degraded links silently staleness internal state ([2603.20285](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.20285)). The custody substrate + Freshness Quotient make staleness evident per field — zero-trust for data.

## 2. Zero-trust on the ACTOR (SYMBOLON / OCULUS)
Trust no actor by default — prove identity, grant least privilege just-in-time, and only while watched. Token-replay and broad keys destroy accountability and non-repudiation in agent delegation chains ([2604.23280](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.23280); [2603.24775](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.24775)); standing privilege is the key enabler of lateral movement, yet JIT adoption is ~1% ([CyberArk 2026](https://www.cyberark.com/press/new-study-only-1-of-organizations-have-fully-adopted-just-in-time-privileged-access-as-ai-driven-identities-rapidly-increase/); [Palo Alto ZSP](https://www.paloaltonetworks.com/cyberpedia/zero-standing-privileges)). SYMBOLON proves who; OCULUS binds privilege to live observation, fail-closed.

## 3. Zero-trust on BEHAVIOR (NEMESIS / EXAMEN)
Trust no agent's self-report — observe from beneath and verify independently. A single injected error cascades across a fleet ([2603.04474](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04474v1)); injection beats SOTA defenses >85% of the time ([2511.05269](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.05269)). NEMESIS watches behavior at the substrate (ground truth); EXAMEN verifies cross-model (author != verifier). No self-attestation.

## 4. Zero-trust on PROVENANCE & GOVERNANCE (ACTA / NOMOS)
Trust no artifact or action without an attestation produced inside the trust boundary, and bind every agent to a runtime-enforced canon. Supply-chain attacks rose to ~88,000/yr ([2308.04898](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2308.04898)); static governance frameworks exhibit an "agentic fitness gap" ([2510.25863](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.25863)). ACTA attests every action immutably; NOMOS enforces conformance continuously, not once.

## 5. Zero-trust as COMMERCIAL INEVITABILITY (the moat becomes the market)
As agentics scale, every failure mode above hits any enterprise running real autonomous agents — not just defense. Zero-trust stops being a premium and becomes the precondition to function. The standard play is build-low-then-harden; ours is build to the zero-trust bar once, for everything. **The moat becomes the market** — and SKIPJACK is first-mover on the posture everyone is forced to adopt.
