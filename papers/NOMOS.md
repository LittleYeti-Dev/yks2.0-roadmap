# White Paper — NOMOS (Governed Agent Spine)

## Use case
An enterprise turns on a fleet of autonomous agents. They act between human review points, tool-using and adaptive. The existing governance program — built for bounded, human-in-the-loop AI — cannot see or constrain them at runtime.

## The problem (evidenced)
- NIST AI RMF and the EU AI Act are **static and principle-based**, while agentic systems are **dynamic and tool-using**, producing a documented **"agentic fitness gap"**; they require continuous runtime oversight, not one-time certification ([AAGATE, arXiv:2510.25863](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.25863)).
- Ethical assurance and governance for agentic AI is an open, unified-framework problem ([AGENTSAFE, arXiv:2512.03180](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.03180)).

## How SKIPJACK solves it
NOMOS is the governed spine: it adopts Ologos's OAgents **conformance levels** and **policy decision point**, adds the zero-trust overlay (two-key gates, autonomy-class matrix, interruption contract), and binds every agent to an explicit **canon** with **runtime** policy enforcement — closing the static-framework gap with continuous, evidence-producing governance rather than point-in-time certification.
